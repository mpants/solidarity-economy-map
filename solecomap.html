Explore Your Living Community

This map is a growing record of the businesses, nonprofits, and public places that provide ways to meet our needs without big corporations. More than a "buy local" movement, it identifies the practices existing in your community that are planting the seeds of a more sustainable people-and-planet economy.

Use the navigation at the top right to zoom to a specific region, or check out the drop-down boxes that let you search by Need you want an alternative way to meet, by economic Practices that these groups adopt, or by Type of organization.

Some suggested uses: use the map to try and meet all your needs with only used and free goods for a month, or get all of your food from gardens, CSAs, and co-ops. Next time you have a need, check here before going to a corporation and see how many of your needs can be met by your own neighbors.

Note: This map is still being populated and so won't be very useful just yet. Expect to see it much more full very soon! If you'd like to help or you have something to say about a result, e-mail map@solidaritypiedmont.org .

Mapping Another Economy

This map seeks to reveal the places in our community that support the values of sustainability and social justice. We are trying to trace the shape of a more humane economy and encourage people to look to these sources to meet their daily needs. Collectively, we call the pieces of this network the Piedmont Solidarity Economy. What is a solidarity economy?

Using the Map

The places on the map are discoverable by the Need they meet, by the Practices they engage in, and by the Type of place they are. Use the zoom controls at the top to highlight a certain region, and click the options on the left to see the examples in that area. Click a dot to see information about the place.

Check our our growing directory.

If you'd like your organization to be listed, please submit it with this form. For any other questions, e-mail us.
