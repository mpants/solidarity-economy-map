Using the Map

Like a search engine, there is no wrong way to use the Solidarity Economy Map. Some suggested uses include:

  • Identifying places that sell reused or re-purposed goods
  • Mapping the cooperatively owned or managed businesses in the region
  • "Buying local," by searching for locally owned businesses
  • Discovering and supporting places that pay a living wage to employees
  • Finding alternative sources of local media, such as community radio or zines
  • Locating and comparing the practices of credit unions in your area
  • Find out who accepts local currencies, such as the NC Plenty

Finding Local Food With the Soleco Map

As an example of how to use the map, let's look up locally grown food!

1. Select what you're looking for - for example, "Food."

 

2. Select what value or practice you want the map to highlight. Here, we want to see places that have locally-grown food.

3. The map now shows places that provide Food and practices Local Sourcing of Food. You can select one from the List on the left, or the Icons on the map to find out where and what a place is, as well as what other positive economic practices it participates in.

Those are the basics! Remember that the map is growing all the time, and is only as good as the listings everyone contributes to it. If you want to make a more useful map, please help us to Add a Place.
